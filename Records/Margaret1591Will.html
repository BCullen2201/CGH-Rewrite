Will of Margaret Robinson
Late of Upton
Written Feb 8, 1591

Jim Cullen

Introduction

Margaret Robinson had obvious connections to Upton, Nottinghamshire and to the Cullen family of Upton. It is included here to show that there is direct evidence of contemporaries of Richard Cullen who lived in Upton at an early date. Richard Cullen died in Upton and was buried in Upton churchyard in 1580 next to his father and his first wife.

Transcription

Nuncupative Will of Margaret Robinson, Spinster Late of Upton, Nottinghamshire
Written Feb 8, 1591 & Probated Mar 18, 1591

Memorandum that upon Shrove Tuesday, the 8th of February 1591, Margaret Robinson, late of Upton near Southwell, spinster, deceased—being then sick in body but of good and perfect memory—did by word of mouth make and declare her last will as hereafter follows, viz: Katherine Robinson, her mother, asking her how she would bestow her goods, she the said Margaret answered and said: "I give to my brother Henry Robinson 20s. in money, in the stand, the choice of a quilt or coverlet, and my sister Mary to have the coverlet or quilt that he leaves. I give unto my sister Mary's children (meaning the children of Mary Gibson) five shillings to either of them, half a crown apiece, that is in their father's hands. I give to my brother Robert Cullen's child, John Cullen, his bed. I give unto my mother my cow and the rest of my goods." Then and there being present Robert Gibson, Thomas Cullen, Katherine Robinson, and Henry Robinson, whom she desired to bear witness that this was her last will. And on the 18th day of the month of March, in the year of our Lord, according to the computation of the English Church 1591, at Southwell, before the discreet man Mr. John Cooper, cleric of the venerable Chapter of the Collegiate Church of the Blessed Mary the Virgin of Southwell in the county of Nottingham, Vicar General in spirituals, in the presence of me, John Martiall, Notary Public and Registrar, this last nuncupative will was proved by Robert Gibson, witness named in the same, sworn in form of law. And administration of all and singular the goods, rights, credits, and chattels which were of the said Margaret Robinson, deceased at the time of her death, being within the jurisdiction of the said Chapter, was and is committed to the said Henry Robinson, the natural and lawful brother of the said deceased, to fulfill the same last will, being sworn in form of law, saving the right of anyone whomsoever, providing security, and an inventory to be exhibited by him, the administrator, by virtue of his oath, etc.

The said 18th day of the month of March 1591, administration of all and singular the goods, rights, credits, and chattels which were of Katherine Robinson, late of Upton near Southwell in the county of Nottingham, widow, deceased intestate as it appears, at the time of her death being within the jurisdiction of the venerable Chapter of the Collegiate Church of the Blessed Mary the Virgin of Southwell aforesaid, is committed to Henry Robinson, the natural and lawful son of the intestate herself, sworn in form of law by the authority of the said Chapter, saving the right of anyone whomsoever, an inventory to be exhibited by him by virtue of his oath and security provided, etc..

A nuncupative will, given orally during a time of immediate peril of a final or sudden illness, was subject to some very strict legal requirements, which will be become important in a little later. Margaret evidently passed due to some illness or injury at a relatively young age. Margaret's mother, Katherine Robinson, was named as Executrix. This family is interesting in that the mother, Katherine Robinson, had a previous husband who was a Cullen. For whatever reason, that marriage came to an end and Katherine remarried to another husband who was a Robinson. For this reason, the family members mentioned in the will are a little confusing. Margaret names a brother Robert Cullen who was old enough to have a son John Cullen who was also mentioned in the will. This Robert Cullen is unknown but is obviously connected to the Cullen family of Upton. Margaret also has a brother Henrie Robinson and a sister Marie Gibson. It's not known if Marie was originally a Robinson or a Cullen. We have another Cullen connection when we come to the list of witnesses. The legal requirements for the witnesses were strict in that they could have no interest in the inheritances of the deceased. The witnesses in this case were: Robert Gibson, Thomas Cullen, Katherine Robinson, and Henrie Robinson. Robert Gibson was evidently Marie's husband. Katherine Robinson was very likely Margaret's mother. Henrie Robinson was Margaret's brother. Thomas Cullen - we're not sure of - but here we are with another Cullen mentioned in the will of Margaret Robinson. If this was Thomas, son of Richard Cullen, then he would have been about 24 years old at the time. It's just as likely though, and perhaps more so, that this could be the Thomas Cullen mentioned in the documents for Collie's Charity.
