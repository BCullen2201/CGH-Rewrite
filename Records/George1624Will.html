Will of George Kitchen, Husbandman of Upton
Written Feb 12, 1624, Proved Mar 1, 1624

Jim Cullen

The Kitchen/Kitching family is one that has deep and long respected standing in Upton. George Kitchen was a peer of Thomas Cullen the Churchwarden. In fact, they were brothers-in-law. George Kitchen was the brother of Agnes Kitchen whom Thomas Cullen married in 1593. George still calls Thomas his brother though Thomas Cullen's marriage to Agnes Kitchen ended in 1611 when Agnes passed away. I've included the will of George Kitchen for this reason and for the revealing of several other family connections as well. We now understand that Agnes had a brother George Kitchen and also another brother William Kitchen of Rolleston. One of the witnesses to the will of George Kitchen is Thomas Cullen and we see that, once again, Thomas did not sign his signature but placed his mark (TC). We see the same circle off friends, families, and neighbors that we see in nearly every document having to do with the Cullen family.

There is a word I have left in brackets, [neuie], which is a word describing a relationship. Loosely it means neice (or nephew) but it can also be a term applied to other relationships as well. These terms were a little loose as compared to our understandings today. Notice that George's inventory totals about £29 which is a reasonable amount for a Husbandman or Yeoman in that area at that time. Though Thomas Cullen and George Kitchen were peers, the relative wealth of the Cullen family is remarkable. Thomas Cullen's inventory totalled about £182 in 1628.
