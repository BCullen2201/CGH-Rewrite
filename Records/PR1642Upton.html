Protestation Return
Upton, Nottinghamshire 1642

Jim Cullen

Introduction

This is a transcription of the Protestation Return for Upton, Nottinghamshire in 1642. Due to tensions between King Charles I and Parliament in the years before the Civil War, an oath or Protestation was drafted, requiring every adult male over the age of 18 in Great Britain and Wales to maintain and defend the true Reformed Protestant Religion.

The oath was initially signed by all Members of the House of Commons and the House of Lords in May 1641. The order was then sent throughout the kingdom, in January 1641/42 to the local officials (ministers, churchwardens, constables) and the men of every perish would gather, usually at the local church, to swear the oath and have their names recorded in a list. Those who refused to sign (recussant) were recorded in a separate list, identifying the dissenters in the parish community. Since this was to include every male over 18 in the kingdom or about 30% of the population, the Protestation Returns are considered one of the best census substitues between the Lay Subsidies and the Census of 1841.

This was a rushed affair so there was no set format to the returns. Some returns were dated and some were not. Some were signed by the local officials to attest to the accuracy of the Protestation and some were not. On the return for Upton, there was no date nor any signatures by the local officials. At the end of the parchment there was the simple phrase: "There are not any in our towne but hath willingly taken this p(ro)mist vow and p(ro)testation", meaning no dissenters (recussants) had been identified.

I find the Protestation Returns very interesting since the list of names represent a cross section of the families in the parish. I'm curious enough that I will provide transcriptions here, as time allows, for other parishes surrounding Upton whether there are Cullens present on the list or not. The Oath of Allegiance

While looking through the Protestation Returns for the parishes in the same general area as Upton, I came across the return for the Parish of Kelham. It was dated March 5, 1641 (or 1642 on the new calendar) and had the oath written out right on the return itself:

Kelham. March 5, 1641 {1642 new calendar} The Protestation.

I [A.B.] doe in the presence of Almightie God, promise, vow, and protest to maintaine and defend, as fare as lawfully I may, with my life, power and estate the true reformed protestant Religion expressed in the doctrine of the Church of England, against all popery and popish innovations within this realme, contrary to the same doctrine, and accordinge to the duty of my allegiance his majesties Royall person, honour and estate, as alsoe the power and priviledges of parliament, the lawfull rights and liberties of the subject, and every person that makes this protestation, in whatsoeuer he shall doe in the lawfull pursuance of the same, and to my power and as far as lawfully I may, I will oppose and by all good wayes and meanes endeavour to bringe to condigne punishment, all such as shall either by force, practice, counsells, plots, conspiracies, or otherwise, doe any thinge to the contrary of any thinge in this present protestation contained. And further that I shall in all just and honorable wayes endeavour to preserve the union and peace, betweene the three kingdomes of England, Scotland, and Ireland, and neither for hope, feare nor other respect shall relinquish this promise, vow, and protestation.

Source Reference: YHL/PO/JO/10/1/101/94, Protestation Return: Nottinghamshire: Thurgarton Hundred – Kelham
